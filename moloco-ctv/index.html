FTC proposes new ACR disclosure rules for smart TV manufacturers

6/10
◆ neutral Published Mar 23, 2026 • Captured Mar 27, 2026
ACR restrictions affect all CTV players but Moloco's first-party optimization approach is relatively insulated

The FTC issued a notice of proposed rulemaking that would require smart TV manufacturers to provide clear, prominent disclosure of Automatic Content Recognition (ACR) data collection and give consumers a simple opt-out mechanism at device setup, not buried in settings menus.

Hot Take

This is a medium-risk regulatory signal. ACR data powers much of the household-level targeting and attribution that makes CTV performance measurable. If opt-out rates increase due to prominent disclosure, the data pool shrinks — but it shrinks for everyone equally. Moloco's advantage: ML models trained on first-party conversion data are less dependent on ACR than competitors relying on third-party audience segments. Position this as a competitive moat: 'Our models optimize on your outcomes, not borrowed device data.'
